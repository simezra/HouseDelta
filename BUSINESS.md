# HouseDelta — Business Plan & Strategy

*Living document. Last updated: 2026-03-24.*

---

## Vision

HouseDelta becomes the go-to platform for Canadians making housing decisions — starting with Toronto, expanding nationally. We make the complex math of homeownership transparent and use that trust to connect users with the right financial products and professionals.

**Core insight:** People making $500K–$2M decisions are underserved by existing tools. Bank calculators are biased (they want to sell you a mortgage). Generic calculators are shallow (P&I only). Nobody shows you the househacking math. HouseDelta is the first tool that tells the full truth — and that trust is the product.

## Revenue Streams

### 1. Lead Generation (Primary — Near-term)

Users who've spent 10+ minutes modeling their housing costs are the highest-intent leads in real estate. They know their numbers, they're actively planning, and they're ready to talk to a professional.

**How it works:**
- After exploring the calculator, offer: *"Want a mortgage broker to review your numbers? Get matched for free."*
- Collect: name, email, phone, and their calculator state (home price, down payment, rate, timeline)
- Sell qualified leads to mortgage brokers/agents at $30–$100/lead (Toronto market rates)
- Users opt in explicitly — no dark patterns, trust is the brand

**Lead types to sell:**
| Lead Type | Buyer | Est. Value |
|-----------|-------|------------|
| Mortgage pre-approval | Mortgage brokers | $50–$100/lead |
| First-time buyer consultation | Real estate agents | $30–$75/lead |
| Refinance inquiry | Mortgage brokers | $40–$80/lead |
| Investment property / househack | Investment-focused agents | $75–$150/lead |
| Insurance quote | Home insurance providers | $15–$30/lead |

**Implementation phases:**
1. **v1 — Simple form:** "Get a free consultation" CTA → collects info → we manually connect with partner brokers
2. **v2 — Matching system:** User preferences matched to broker profiles, automated email intros
3. **v3 — Marketplace:** Brokers bid on leads, users see competing offers

### 2. Affiliate Partnerships (Near-term)

Partner with mortgage comparison sites and lenders for referral commissions.

- Link to rate comparison tools (e.g., Ratehub, Nesto, Pine) with affiliate tracking
- "See today's best rates" button that links to partner with referral code
- Commission: $200–$500 per funded mortgage (industry standard for Canadian affiliates)
- Lower friction than lead gen — user clicks through, we get paid if they convert

### 3. Sponsored Rate Integration (Medium-term)

Let mortgage lenders pay to display their rates directly in the calculator.

- "Current rates from our partners" section showing real rates from 3–5 lenders
- Lenders pay for placement (CPM or per-click)
- User benefit: they see real rates instead of guessing — improves tool accuracy
- Revenue: $5–$15 CPM, or $2–$5 per click-through
- Important: clearly label as sponsored, maintain trust

### 4. Premium Features / Freemium (Medium-term)

Core calculator stays free forever. Premium tier adds:

| Free | Premium ($8–$15/mo or $79/yr) |
|------|-------------------------------|
| All calculators | Save & compare scenarios |
| Basic comparison | PDF report generation |
| Default assumptions | Custom cost assumptions |
| | Historical rate overlay |
| | Neighbourhood-level data (avg prices, taxes, appreciation) |
| | Email alerts when rates drop to your target |
| | Multi-property portfolio modeling |

### 5. White-Label / B2B Licensing (Longer-term)

License the calculator engine to:
- Mortgage brokerages (embed on their site, branded)
- Real estate teams (give to clients during consultations)
- Financial advisors (include in financial planning tools)
- Pricing: $200–$500/mo per brokerage, volume discounts

### 6. Content & SEO Monetization (Ongoing)

Build authority content around the calculator:
- "True cost of buying in [neighbourhood]" articles (SEO)
- "Househacking in Toronto: A Complete Guide" (long-tail)
- Email newsletter with rate updates and market insights
- Monetize via display ads (Mediavine/AdThrive at scale) and affiliate links in content

## Lead Capture — Implementation Plan

### Touchpoints for opt-in (non-intrusive, trust-first):

1. **After calculator engagement** — Once user has adjusted 3+ sliders, show a subtle banner: *"Want a broker to review your numbers?"*
2. **Comparison section CTA** — After seeing rent vs buy vs househack results: *"Ready to explore your options? Get matched with a Toronto mortgage specialist."*
3. **Insights section** — After personalized insights: *"Get a personalized action plan from a licensed broker — free, no obligation."*
4. **Exit intent** — On scroll-to-top or tab switch: *"Before you go — save your scenario and get expert advice."*

### Lead capture form (keep it short):
- First name
- Email
- Phone (optional)
- "I'm looking to..." (dropdown: Buy first home / Refinance / Invest / Househack / Just exploring)
- Auto-attach: their calculator state (home price, rate, down payment, scenario winner)
- Consent checkbox: "I agree to be contacted by a HouseDelta partner broker"

### What makes our leads valuable:
- **Pre-qualified by data**: we know their price range, down payment, and preferred strategy
- **High intent**: they've spent time modeling scenarios, not just browsing
- **Toronto-specific**: geo-targeted, relevant to local brokers
- **Warm context**: broker receives the user's calculator state, can have an informed first conversation

## Market Sizing (Toronto)

- ~100,000 home sales/year in GTA
- ~300,000+ people actively searching/researching at any time
- Average mortgage broker pays $50–$100 per qualified lead
- If we capture 1% of active researchers (3,000 users/mo) and 5% opt in to lead gen:
  - 150 leads/mo × $50 = **$7,500/mo revenue** from lead gen alone
  - Plus affiliate, sponsored rates, premium — realistic target: **$15–$25K/mo within 12 months**

## Monetization Sequence

**Strategy: Build trust and users first, monetize with credibility.**

Users → Reviews → Credibility → Premium revenue → Broker partnerships → Scale

### Phase 1 — Free tool, build audience (Now → Summer 2026)
- Ship polished calculator, optimize for SEO
- Organic growth: Reddit, Twitter/X, Toronto homebuyer communities
- Collect emails via "save your scenario" (free, no paywall yet)
- Goal: **5,000 monthly users, 500 email subscribers**

### Phase 2 — Freemium launch (Summer 2026)
- User accounts, save & compare scenarios
- Premium tier ($8–$15/mo): PDF reports, neighbourhood data, rate alerts, multi-property modeling
- Actively collect reviews and testimonials from free users
- Content/SEO blog: "True cost of buying in [neighbourhood]" articles
- Goal: **200 paying subscribers ($2–$3K/mo), strong reviews/social proof**

### Phase 3 — Affiliate & sponsored rates (Fall 2026)
- Affiliate links to Ratehub/Nesto/Pine ("See today's best rates")
- Sponsored rate placements from lenders inside the calculator
- Newsletter with rate updates + affiliate links
- Goal: **$3–$5K/mo affiliate + sponsorship revenue**

### Phase 4 — Lead generation (Early 2027)
- By now we have users, reviews, and credibility to attract broker partners
- Lead capture forms at high-intent touchpoints (post-calculator, post-comparison)
- Partner with 3–5 Toronto mortgage brokers, fixed fee per lead
- Goal: **150 leads/mo × $50 = $7.5K/mo, total revenue $15–$20K/mo**

### Phase 5 — Scale (2027+)
- Broker marketplace (brokers bid on leads)
- Expand to Vancouver, Ottawa, national
- White-label licensing to brokerages ($200–$500/mo each)
- API for financial advisors and real estate teams
- Goal: **$50K+/mo revenue, multiple cities**
